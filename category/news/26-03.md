<h4 class="section-title underline">New Positions</h4> 
    
<p align="justify">The section of Molecular Ecology and Evolution at the Globe Institute, University of Copenhagen, invites applications for<a href="https://employment.ku.dk/faculty/?show=157125"><b> a 1.5 year postdoctoral position in ancient environmental multiomics</b></a>, starting 1 May 2026, or as soon as possible thereafter.
</p>
<p align="justify">As a postdoctoral researcher, you'll work at the cutting edge of ancient environmental genomics and human evolutionary biology, analyzing multi-omic datasets from Neanderthal and early modern human coprolites and faecal-rich sediments. You will apply computational and statistical approaches to reconstruct ancient environments and gut microbiomes, characterize host-microbe interactions, and test how these communities responded to dietary shifts and environmental pressures during the critical period of Neanderthal-AMH transition in Westernmost Europe
</p>
<p align="justify">Deadline for applications: <b> 5 March, 23.59pm CET</b>. If you'd like more information, please contact <a href="mailtoainara.sistiaga@sund.ku.dk"><b>Ainara Sistiaga</b></a> or <a href="mailto:mwpedersen@sund.ku.dk"><b>Mikkel Winther Pedersen</b></a> .</p>

 <p align="justify">The Postdoc blends hands-on laboratory work, bioinformatic analysis, academic writing, and meaningful collaboration with students, researchers, iwi, and partner organisations.
